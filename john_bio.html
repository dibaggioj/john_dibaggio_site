About Me

Education

I attended the University of Kansas from the fall of 2009 to the spring of 2013 and received a Bachelor of Science degree in Chemical Engineering from the university in May 2013. I received departmental honors, completed the university honors program, and graduated with distinction. With interests in patents and intellectual property, I went on to study law at the University of Kansas School of Law in the fall of 2014, until deciding near the end of my first semester to become more actively involved in engineering and technology.

In 2011, I became a member of the Zeta Chapter of Theta Tau Professional Engineering Fraternity.

Engineering Projects

Kinetics and Solvent Effects in Ionic Liquid Synthesis

I analyzed kinetics and solvent effects in ionic liquid syntheses at varying temperatures necessary for process design and optimization, by using an NMR spectroscopy approach. I interpreted spectral data using TopSpin and MestReNova softwares.

Biomass Processing Using Ionic Liquids

I implemented ionic liquids in biomass processing for breaking down and recovering cellulose to use as potentially viable energy source, by developing an accurate NMR method for calculating compositions, opposed to a less effective filtration approach.

Grundfos Challenge United States 2012

In November 2012, I worked in a three-person team of engineering students from the University of Kansas to compete in the North American regional Grundfos Challenge held in Kansas City by Grundfos, the global leader in manufacturing high quality and high efficiency pumps and designing and implementing innovative engineering solutions. In the competition, Grundfos presented all students with the challenge of developing a sustainable solution that addresses a need in the water industry and increases Grundfos’s market share within the water industry. Some requirements were to increase reliability and security, decrease cost on consumers, reduce energy consumption, and reduce water loss. The University of Kansas team was selected as a winner in the competition and went on to compete in the final, international competition held in Denmark.

Grundfos Challenge International 2013

In March 2013, I worked in a three-person team of engineering students from the University of Kansas to compete in the International Grundfos Challenge held in Bjerringbro, Denmark by Grundfos, the global leader in manufacturing high quality and high efficiency pumps and designing and implementing innovative engineering solutions. In the competition, Grundfos presented all students with the challenge of addressing a problem faced by a specified market segment in the water industry. The solution the University of Kansas team proposed included a short-term strategy with a vision for sustainability over the next fifty years. The solution appealed to the company’s values of sustainability, openness and trustworthiness, focus on people, independence, partnership, and relentless ambition, all while keeping in mind the company’s overriding purpose and vision.

Chemical Engineering Senior Design I

I designed an innovative, environmentally benign process for the production of medical-grade hydrogen peroxide, which utilized cutting-edge research in the field of high-pressure carbon dioxide.

Chemical Engineering Senior Design II

I worked in a person-person team of chemical engineering students to complete the 2013 AIChE National Student Design Competition. The project required both the design of a process for the production of biofuels from biomass and an economic comparison with a similar process containing a different reaction scheme.

Biomedical Product Development

For a graduate-level course, I worked in a three-person team of engineering students at the University of Kansas to develop a potential startup company based on a current research project by a University of Kansas engineering professor and a University of Kansas professor of medicine. Deliverables of project included a NIH Fast-Track SBIR proposal containing a research plan and a commercialization plan, a venture capital pitch slide deck and presentation, multiple iterations of a business model canvas, multiple elevator pitches, and a design history file for the project.

The Gigabots

I am working with the Founder and CEO of a Think Big Accelerator startup named Big Bang on a Mozilla Gigabit Community Fund project, funded by the National Science Foundation. The mission of The Gigabots is to bring connected robotics and devices to classrooms using existing educational robotics platforms. The robot firmware and the web-based dashboard dashboard both use Big Bang, which is a realtime data synchronization platform. Check out The Gigabots website and follow The Gigabots on Twitter for more updates.
